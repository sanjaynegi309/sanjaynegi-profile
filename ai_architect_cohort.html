What Does a GenAI Architect Actually Do?

The role connects business strategy, AI use cases, data, models, applications, platforms, security, governance, operations and delivery teams. A credible GenAI Architect is not just the most senior developer, not a cloud architect adding an LLM API box, and not someone who recommends tools without understanding operating constraints.

→
Choose platform and integration patterns
→
Establish security, evaluation and governance
→

→
Review production readiness
→
Measure, learn and evolve the platform

You are not only designing one AI application. You are designing the foundation that allows many AI applications to be built, governed and operated responsibly.

An AI Engineer builds the system. An FDE drives the customer outcome. A GenAI Architect defines the technical foundation, standards and guardrails that allow systems and teams to scale.

GenAI Architect-Specific FAQs

How the senior architecture path works in practice.

What exactly does a GenAI Architect do?
A GenAI Architect defines the technical foundation, standards, governance and operating model that help multiple teams build AI systems safely and at scale.
How is a GenAI Architect different from an AI Engineer?
An AI Engineer builds and deploys production AI systems. A GenAI Architect defines platform architecture, standards, governance and technical direction across systems and teams.
How is a GenAI Architect different from a Forward Deployed Engineer?
An FDE works with customers to discover the right problem and drive production adoption. A GenAI Architect creates the enterprise foundation that many teams and use cases can build on.
How is this different from a traditional solution or enterprise architecture program?
It adds GenAI-specific model strategy, RAG, agents, evaluation, LLMOps, governance, security and responsible AI architecture to traditional architecture judgement.
Do I need to be an architect already?
No. You should have substantial production software or enterprise systems experience, but you do not need to already hold an architect title.
Do I need hands-on AI Engineering experience before joining?
Hands-on AI experience is useful but not always mandatory. The path remains technically grounded and helps you understand implementation deeply enough to guide and challenge teams.
Will I still write code?
Architects should understand implementation reality. You may work through technical examples, but the main output is architecture, governance and operating-model evidence.
How much LLM Engineering depth is included?
Enough to make model, gateway, routing, context, cost, latency, privacy and reliability decisions credibly.
Will we cover enterprise RAG architecture?
Yes. Enterprise RAG architecture is a dedicated module covering ingestion, retrieval, access control, tenant isolation, evaluation and observability.
Will we cover agentic AI architecture?
Yes. Agentic AI is covered through agent versus workflow decisions, tool permissions, human approval, auditability, observability, failure recovery and enterprise risk.
Will OpenAI Agents SDK, LangGraph, CrewAI, Google ADK and LangChain be included?
Yes, these are represented as implementation tools and architecture options. Frameworks are not the centre of the role.
Will we cover MLOps and LLMOps?
Yes. The platform operations module covers LLMOps practices, MLOps foundations, deployment, observability, quality, cost, latency, reliability and incident management.
Will we cover security, governance and Responsible AI?
Yes. Security, privacy, responsible AI governance, model and use-case inventory, risk classification, review gates, auditability and incident response are central.
Will we cover architecture on cloud platforms?
Yes. Cloud, hybrid and multi-cloud considerations, private connectivity, deployment topologies, scalability and resilience are included at an architecture level.
Will we cover model and vendor selection?
Yes. The curriculum covers hosted versus self-hosted, commercial versus open models, model gateways, routing, fallback, vendor lock-in and build-versus-buy decisions.
Will we cover build-versus-buy decisions?
Yes. Build-versus-buy decisions appear in model strategy, platform strategy, vendor evaluation and executive communication.
Will we create reference architectures and architecture decision records?
Yes. Reference architectures, architecture decision records and design-review narratives are core artifacts.
Is this suitable for engineering managers and technical leads?
Yes, especially if you remain close to architecture and want to guide teams through GenAI decisions, standards and trade-offs.
Will this help me prepare for architecture reviews and interviews?
Yes. You will build architecture artifacts, trade-off narratives and capstone evidence that can support architecture reviews, interviews and internal role conversations.
What kind of enterprise capstone will I complete?
You will create and defend a GenAI platform and governance blueprint covering target-state architecture, model strategy, RAG, agents, security, evaluation, governance, LLMOps, integration, cost, risk and adoption roadmap.
